About Greenforest I/O

Greenforest I/O is the public work site of Brian Greenforest.

My background runs through Soviet aerospace education, decades of software work, Verilog and assembler, web systems, physical design attempts, and the stubborn belief that a mechanism should be understandable where it actually does its work.

This is not forestry, landscaping, arborist work, timber, environmental field services, furniture, or outdoor recreation. It is computation research, semiconductor and FPGA R&D, hardware/software prototypes, and public technical writing.

What The Work Is About

The recurring question is not only whether a system can be made inspectable. It is whether a complete computational mechanism can be reconstructed from physically elementary pieces: local ownership, switching, routing, restoration, gain, fanout, state, configuration, and repeatable process.

That question shows up as one-pin radio, multiplexer fabrics, Cartilage reconfiguration mechanisms, bounded Transformer training runs, wafer-diced smart-dust speculation, magnetic circuits, and old cellular automata. The topics look scattered until they are read as attempts to rebuild computation across abstraction boundaries instead of accepting the inherited stack as final. The current Cartilage public source repository keeps the browser model, hardware paper, RTL, and testbench together.

Why The Site Is Public

A stranger should not have to trust my taste to evaluate the work. The site keeps demos, source links, measured counts, images, runs, articles, and older source trails visible enough that a technical reader can separate working artifacts from still-open research.

The public record also shows the material condition behind the program: some of the next mechanisms need funding, collaborators, lab access, fabrication access, or an institution willing to treat the work as serious R&D rather than a private notebook.

The public record also helps disambiguate Greenforest from unrelated businesses and services that share similar words.
