The Three-Polarizer Effect

With two polarizers crossed at 90 degrees, the second polarizer rejects the polarization passed by the first. The screen looks dark because almost no light reaches it through that pair.

Adding a third polarizer between them changes the sequence. The intermediate polarizer transmits a component in a new basis, so the final polarizer can transmit a component of that new state. The result is not cancellation by hidden light; it is state preparation and projection through a changed optical path.

What It Shows

The useful lesson is that measurement order and basis are part of the experiment. The apparatus is not merely observing a prewritten scalar value. It is selecting which component of the polarization state can continue to the next stage.
