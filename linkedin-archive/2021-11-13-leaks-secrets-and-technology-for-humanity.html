Technology Secrecy and Manufacturing Concentration
November 13, 2021

Technology-access noteOriginally posted 2021-11-13; rewritten here around public technical knowledge and manufacturing concentration.

Included: technology education, open-source business, semiconductor concentration, public technical knowledge, and the boundary between security secrets and teachable engineering.

What Should Be Public

Security information and technical education are different categories. Some operational details should remain protected. But the engineering knowledge required to build, test, repair, and improve useful technology should not be trapped behind needless secrecy.

When a society cannot teach or reproduce important technical processes, the dependency becomes structural. Semiconductor manufacturing is the hard example: design knowledge, process knowledge, equipment, materials, and metrology all affect whether a community can build trusted active devices.

Concentration Is a Technical Risk

Chip manufacturing concentration is not only an economic issue. It affects supply resilience, education, trust, iteration speed, and who can verify what was built.

Open-source businesses show that public technical work and commercial value can coexist. The useful boundary is not "everything secret" or "everything leaked." The useful boundary is public enough for learning, reproduction, and improvement while still protecting legitimate operational security.

Useful Scope

This note belongs with the maker-fab and microelectronics-education articles. The practical question is what knowledge, equipment, process recipes, and test methods have to be shareable before local builders can own more of the active-device stack.
