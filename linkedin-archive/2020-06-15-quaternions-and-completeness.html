The Mathematical Question

Complex Hilbert spaces are standard in quantum mechanics and many parts of applied mathematics. Quaternionic Hilbert spaces ask what changes when the scalar field is noncommutative and carries more structure than the complex plane.

The linked paper by Ghiloni, Moretti, and Perotti develops continuous slice functional calculus in quaternionic Hilbert spaces. That kind of machinery matters because operators, spectra, and functional calculus are where abstract number systems become usable in analysis and physics.

Why It Matters

The value is not a claim that quaternions replace every complex-number model. The value is having precise tools for the cases where quaternionic structure is the right mathematical object: rotations, noncommutative scalar behavior, and operator theory that cannot be treated as ordinary complex analysis with different notation.
