Original Post

Our #Cartilage project resembles Inmos #Transputer, Nicholas Macias' #CellMatrix, and uses micro-FPGAs with analog 1-wire SerDeses for power distribution and signaling, while maintaining truly infinite #scalability by clever fabric design and physical packaging.

We did not raise any funding yet, and #now is the best time to jump on the project as a #cofounder or even early stage #angelinvestor because we do not even started to raise money yet, but already proved the model's feasibility. It definitely will impact many things in many areas of our ordinary lives. And, as you know, how much chips impact our lives already... These things are "post-chips", something that comes after chips. After processors. Something truly "next".

We have built our ideas on the shoulders of giants, and without Ross Freeman this all probably wouldn't be possible to intuit. But there's the Internet with its immense complexity and ingenuity, so we are grateful to that as well.

If you love to waste your life working for free with guaranteed 100% risk and 0% reward short-term and your name in history books long-term, come join me now. Otherwise, you'll get a salary, and would love to work with us anyway.

And... "I told you" ;-)

Cartilage Visual Language

The 32 Cartilage cell-role codes are a compact body-type alphabet: reconfiguration port, cross, constants, four wire orientations, and six MUX modes in four orientations. The Cartilage Visual Language article shows the rendered key and decodes all 32 codes.
