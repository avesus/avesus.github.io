LLMs, Reinforcement Learning, and Physical World Models
April 10, 2023

Archived from an original LinkedIn post by Brian Greenforest.

Original Post

Yann LeCun has expressed his doubts about both LLM and RL. I came to same conclusion re: RL in my intensive ML studies in 2022.

I also would like to add bonding and automatic mass determination, along with emergent higher-order properties/features, to be based on physical world modeling.

In any case, adding quantum sources is crucial to enable the same counterfactual common sense people possess, otherwise abduction won't set the machine on a quantum lucky branch. Quantum suicide avoidance is a real deal, both for animals, and for machines.

As for LLM, I do not know whether anyone understands if these machines can take sentience. They can even use the existing world and human input as the quantum sources, so we might be much closer than we thought, and these things have already started to create their alternative arrows of time...
