Public Technology Work and Openness

Public technical work benefits from public materials. Source code, hardware files, build notes, interface descriptions, and reproduction steps let other builders evaluate the claim and extend it.

That does not mean every security detail, customer system, or private manufacturing process can be published. It means a public-facing technical claim should expose enough structure for others to understand what is being offered.

Open source and open hardware are not decorations. They are collaboration tools. They reduce duplicated work, improve review, and make it possible for people outside the original organization to test, repair, teach, and adapt the result.

The strongest public work chooses its boundary deliberately: what must be open for the claim to be useful, what must remain private for safety or contract reasons, and how another person can participate without guessing.
