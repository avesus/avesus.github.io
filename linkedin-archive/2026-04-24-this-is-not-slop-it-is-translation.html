Full article: Writing Assistance and Published Work explains the site rule: assistance can shape drafts, but responsibility stays with the published page and its source material.

Original Post Context

The original post made a practical distinction: assisted drafting can turn rough notes into clearer public text, but it should structure ideas that already exist rather than invent the work.

The useful public rule is simple. If assistance makes a page easier to read while keeping sources, artifacts, claims, and limits attached, it helps. If it makes a weak page sound stronger than the work behind it, it gets in the way.
