My Why Is Tinkerer-Scale Control Circuitry
July 6, 2022

Tinkerer-scale control circuitry is about bringing active machine behavior closer to the people building, testing, and improving it.

Publication noteOriginally posted 2022-07-06.

Included: the original argument for local access to control circuitry and short build/test loops.

Boundary: this is a public argument, not a delivered local active-device toolchain.

Original Post

My "Why" is to enable tinkerers with continuous iteration with ultra short turnaround times. My "Why" is to empower every local community with ability to create control circuitry in any sophisticated way. Without depending on CPUs, GPUs, microprocessors, even FPGAs. I want give power to people. I want us to not even wait for PCBs or chips to arrive. Something VLSI but without classical fab model. Something scalable. Something massive.

Do you want to start a movement?

Control Boundary

The practical question is which active behavior can be made local first: sensing, switching, gain, restoration, timing, actuation, or test. A useful toolchain starts where builders can measure and repeat the behavior themselves.
