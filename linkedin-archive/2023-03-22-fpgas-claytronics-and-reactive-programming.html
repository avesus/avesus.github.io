Original Post

scott hauck was using FPGAs in Seattle to teach students 20 years ago. These days nobody understands them anymore. While my data science student asked if he needs to prepare for porting ML algorithms to them, I told him to get ready for Claytronics. I spent my whole life inventing these self-assembling machines, but students fail to realize the importance of the massively scalable parallel reactive programming (sweet to learn you and seth goldstein are connected!)

I plan to move back to Seattle to seek an increased interest. Perhaps it's... 20 years a bit late??? 😂😎👍❤️

Cartilage Visual Language

The 32 Cartilage cell-role codes are a compact body-type alphabet: reconfiguration port, cross, constants, four wire orientations, and six MUX modes in four orientations. The Cartilage Visual Language article shows the rendered key and decodes all 32 codes.
