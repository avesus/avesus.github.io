Lattice regularization makes a continuous field problem computable by replacing spacetime with a discrete grid. That turns operators, amplitudes, tensor products, contractions, traces, and transforms into structures that can be calculated directly.

LinkedIn postPosted 2023-07-28.

Discretizing The Field

On a lattice, a field-theory problem can be expressed as values and relationships across discrete sites. Scalars, vectors, spinors, and more complex tensor structures require different bookkeeping, but the practical goal is the same: make the continuous operator problem finite enough to compute.

Measurement tensors are one way to think about what an operator extracts from a state. Tensor products, contractions, and traces describe how pieces combine and reduce. Fourier transforms move the same information into momentum or energy-phase representations when that basis makes the calculation easier.

What Has To Stay Precise

The informal picture only becomes useful when the discretization, boundary conditions, operator definitions, and continuum limit are specified. Otherwise the vocabulary sounds powerful but does not yet define a model that can be checked.
