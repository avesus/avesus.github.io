Life on Earth and Computational Permutations
August 12, 2019

Archived from an original LinkedIn post by Brian Greenforest.

Original Post

Life on Earth can be the only Life in the entire Universe.

If you write code long enough, you can see that. But if you toss coins, you can believe in Aliens, Buddha, The Force, and other Gods... If you're a physicist or mathematician, it depends...

You know what?.. Listen software architects! Listen the voice of thermodynamics! There's a very important reason why permutations never repeat in real computational processes. When data and code are the same. Cellular automata and SKI combinators, Lisp etc. point towards the impossibility of the unique luck we live in!

I am so grateful to be alive and to have you and other people living with me on this Planet. Thank you so much for reading this post! Have a fantastic day!
