Greenforest I/O FAQ

What Is Greenforest I/O?

Greenforest I/O is Brian Greenforest's public research site for inspectable computation, FPGA and browser-native circuit fabrics, one-pin radio, tiny AI, and maker-scale active-device ideas.

Is This A Forestry Or Landscaping Business?

No. Greenforest I/O is not forestry, landscaping, arborist, timber, forest-products, or environmental field services. The word Greenforest is the author/brand name attached to technical computation research.

Who Operates It?

The site is written by Brian Greenforest. Business and technical inquiries go through Solid State Pros LLC.

Where Is It Located?

Greenforest I/O operates from Washington, with Brian Greenforest in Bellingham. Solid State Pros LLC is a Washington LLC incorporated in Blaine, WA.

Mailing address: 250 H ST PMB 567, Blaine, WA 98230-4018, United States.

What Topics Belong Here?

The recurring topics are FPGA and reconfigurable computing, Cartilage, browser-native circuit fabrics, one-pin radio, tiny AI models, semiconductor fabrication boundaries, wafer-diced chiplets, programmable matter, and older cellular-automata experiments.
