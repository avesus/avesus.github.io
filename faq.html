Greenforest I/O FAQ

What Is Greenforest I/O?

Greenforest I/O is Brian Greenforest's public work site for FPGA fabrics, one-pin radio, browser-native GPU computation, learning systems, semiconductor process research, and physical computing mechanisms.

Is This A Forestry Or Landscaping Business?

No. Greenforest I/O is not forestry, landscaping, arborist, timber, forest-products, or environmental field services. The word Greenforest is the author/brand name attached to technical computation research.

Who Operates It?

The site is written by Brian Greenforest. Business, technical, funding, role, and collaboration inquiries go through Solid State Pros LLC.

What Topics Belong Here?

The recurring topics are FPGA and reconfigurable computing, Cartilage, browser-native GPU computation, one-pin radio, bounded learning systems, semiconductor fabrication boundaries, wafer-diced chiplets, programmable matter, magnetics, and older cellular-automata experiments.

Where Can I Inspect The Current Cartilage Implementation?

Start with Cartilage Core: Browser Fabric, RTL, and a 252-Bit Install. The public source repository contains the browser model, hardware paper, SystemVerilog RTL, and Verilator testbench.

How Do I Contact Greenforest I/O?

Email brian@solidstatepros.com.

Related company site: Solid State Pros LLC.

What Opportunities Fit Brian's Work?

Good fits include funded R&D, product architecture, prototype implementation, collaboration with FPGA or semiconductor teams, fabrication or lab access, technical due diligence, and roles involving computational mechanisms across software, hardware, radio, and active-device boundaries.

Technical review can be a useful first step, but it is not the whole offer. The strongest conversations lead toward building, testing, funding, or housing the next mechanism.
