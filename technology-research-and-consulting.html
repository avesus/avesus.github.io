I am most useful when the conventional stack hides the thing that matters: timing, state, routing, signal flow, physical constraint, or the boundary between a simulation and a machine. I reconstruct that layer and carry it far enough for another engineer to run, inspect, challenge, and extend.

Review is one possible first step. The larger fit is R&D architecture, prototype implementation, product-development work, collaboration, funding, fabrication access, or an institutional home for work that cannot be finished as a private collection.

Where I Am Unusually Useful

FPGA system architecture and RTL: work from pin behavior and clocking through arithmetic, signal processing, packet structure, memory, configuration transport, and host control. The public FPGA systems map connects the bubbles-free serial multiplier, one-pin RF receive and transmit paths, a Verilog RMII/ARP reprogrammer, adjacent iCE40 configuration, physical MUX tiles, and Cartilage.

Raw WebGL and GLSL computation: browser-GPU systems built below a rendering framework, with shader compilation, texture-backed state, framebuffer passes, ping-pong updates, and readback exposed as part of the architecture. Inspect the implementation trail.

Reconfigurable systems from logic to space: Cartilage connects LUTs, drivers, event distribution, state machines, timing, routing metal, nested composition, and runtime region replacement. The chapter-by-chapter learning path explains the concepts; Cartilage Core presents the bounded browser-and-RTL proof; the physical MUX tiles make the selector and routing alphabet tangible for EE learners. This is one lane in a broader practice; the advanced R&D partner profile connects it to embedded systems, distributed software, GPU and ML computation, product architecture, and physical research.

These are not unrelated specialties. They are examples of the same contribution: escaping a fixed abstraction when the mechanism beneath it is the real design surface.

Ways To Work With Me

Funded R&D: define the mechanism, build the first runnable artifact, expose the measurements, and separate the physical limit from the tooling problem.

Product and systems architecture: design the boundary between software, hardware, data, tests, deployment, and user-visible behavior so the system can change without becoming opaque.

Prototype implementation: turn a claim, circuit, renderer, FPGA fabric, signal path, or model run into a concrete artifact with source, reproduction steps, and a visible operating envelope.

Collaboration and institutional support: the Cartilage continuation program needs fabrication access, lab discipline, funding, and contributors who want to build the editor, simulator, ownership system, recovery mechanism, and interactive lessons. The Cartilage public source repository is the concrete starting point.

Technical review: useful when the first question is whether a claim, architecture, process boundary, or public evidence page holds up under engineering pressure.

What To Send First

The fastest useful message names the artifact, the boundary, and the opportunity shape.

Send a page, repo, diagram, paper draft, trace, screenshot, demo, measurement, prototype, process idea, or system failure. Then say whether you are asking about funded work, product architecture, a prototype, a collaboration, fabrication access, a role, investment, or a technical review.

I do not need polished pitch material. I need the strongest claim, the part that has to hold up, and what kind of work could happen next if the boundary is real.
