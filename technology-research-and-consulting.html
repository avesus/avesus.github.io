Research And Engineering

Greenforest I/O is not a forest service. It is a technical research site and collaboration surface for inspectable computation.

Business inquiries are handled through Solid State Pros LLC. The useful conversations are usually about hard substrate questions: FPGA fabrics, semiconductor process boundaries, browser-native tools, tiny AI, reconfigurable computation, and programmable matter.

Service/entity pageThis page explains the technical categories behind the public work.

What this does not claim: a packaged product catalog, guaranteed availability, or completed commercial programmable-matter hardware.

Research Areas

FPGA and reconfigurable computing: multiplexer fabrics, edge-visible runs, checked browser artifacts, and spatial computation models.

Semiconductor and active-device R&D: maker-scale fabrication boundaries, wafer-diced chiplets, OECTs, magnetic alternatives, packaging, and trust surfaces.

AI systems: tiny inspectable Transformer training artifacts, local learning machinery, and AI-assisted translation of technical notes into public prose.

Radio and signal processing: stack-stripping experiments such as one-pin FPGA radio and simplified DSP chains.

Useful Engagements

The best fit is not generic web work. It is early technical criticism, R&D architecture, prototype review, semiconductor or FPGA strategy, prior-art discussion, and collaboration around strange but concrete computation systems.

If you are trying to make a computational system more inspectable, more local, or less dependent on hidden inherited machinery, send the sharpest technical boundary first.
