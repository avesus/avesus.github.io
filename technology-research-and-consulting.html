What I Can Help With

Technical critique: I can read a strange architecture, identify the hidden global assumption, separate observed behavior from speculation, and name the next evidence that would make the claim stronger.

Inspectable prototype architecture: I can help turn a difficult computation or hardware idea into a smaller artifact with visible state, explicit boundaries, and reproducible evidence.

FPGA and reconfigurable computing review: I can critique multiplexer fabrics, edge-visible runs, browser-native circuit artifacts, spatial ownership models, and reconfiguration stories.

Semiconductor and active-device boundary research: I can challenge maker-scale fabrication claims, wafer-diced chiplet ideas, OECT or magnetic alternatives, packaging assumptions, and hardware-trust surfaces.

Proof and artifact pages: I can help a technical team explain what was checked, what was generated, what was hand-authored, what remains speculative, and what a stranger can audit without trusting the author.

What To Send First

The fastest useful message is one artifact plus one boundary.

Send a page, repo, diagram, paper draft, trace, screenshot, demo, measurement, or claim. Then tell me the part you most need challenged: performance, physical feasibility, verification, architecture, prior art, fabrication path, trust boundary, or explanation.

I do not need polished pitch material. I need the strongest claim and the place where it might be wrong.
